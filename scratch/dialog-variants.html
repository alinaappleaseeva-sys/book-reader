Skeptic dialog — three layout variants

Each variant uses the same content (Wes #2 from the demo chapter). Compare how clearly the speaker switch reads. All three are intentionally quiet and book-appropriate.

Variant A — Minimal bubbles

Two separate cards with slightly different shapes and colors. Skeptic = navy tint, flush left. Author = olive tint, indented right. No comic-style tails, but the indent and color shift clearly signal a new speaker.

I currently see more than 20 mechanics for value creation, including base mechanics (fix problems, kill Jobs, perform more Jobs with one solution) and composite mechanics (climb to the next-higher Job, take the Job off the person entirely).

Skeptic · Wes

"OK but isn't this just 'understand your customer and make their life easier'? Every UX person already says that. What's actually new here?"

Author

What's new is the unit of analysis. UX talks about flows and screens; AJTBD talks about Jobs and graphs. A flow is a recording of one person doing one thing on one product. A graph is the space of Jobs across solutions, in which your product is one node and your competitors are others.

The same energy-optimization principle, applied at higher levels of the graph and across segments grouped by higher-level Jobs, gives us strategic decisions.

Variant B — Two stacked blocks

Skeptic gets a tinted card. Author gets a plain left-border block (like a reply). No indent — they stack vertically but have clearly different visual weights. The author's block is lighter, more understated.
